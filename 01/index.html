Affirmative form

I work

You work

He works

She works

It works

We work

They work

For he, she and It we normally add –s in the end.

work(s), play(s).

However, verbs that end in –o, s, ch, sh and x is necessary to add –es in the end.

go(goes), watch(watches), fix(fixes).

Verbs that end in y but before have consonant on it, change the –y for –ies.

study(studies), fly (flies).

Negative form

I do not work/ I don’t work

You do not work/ You don’t work.

He does not work/ He doesn’t work.

She does not work/ She doesn’t work.

It does not work/ It doesn’t work.

We do not work/ we don’t work.

They do not work/ They don’t work.

For negative form it is used the auxiliary verbs do or does + not.

The contracted form of do not is don’t and does not is doesn’t.

When we use the auxiliary verb for he, she and it, the main verb remains in the infinitive form.

Interrogative form

Do I work?

Do you work?

Does he work?

Does she work?

Does it work?

Do we work?

Do they work?

For interrogative form it is used the auxiliary verbs do or does before the subject.

When we use the auxiliary verb for he, she and it, the main verb remains in the infinitive form.

When we have information questions, the position of the auxiliary verbs do and does are after the wh questions.

ODBC

ODBC, ou Open Database Connectivity, é um conjunto de classes-modelo para conexão disponível em qualquer linguagem de programação, diferindo-se da JDBC, que é exclusiva para Java.

JDBC

JDBC, ou Java EE Database Connectivity, é uma API (application programming interfaces) que possibilita a interação do código Java com um banco de dados, na qual as instruções SQL são executadas por meio de um driver, composto por um grupo de classes e interfaces desenvolvido em Java. Esse processo possibilita a execução de instruções SQLs que se conectarão com a base de dados, permitindo a manipulação dos valores.
