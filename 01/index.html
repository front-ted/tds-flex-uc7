John works at the Apple company. Read about his routine during his journey and answer the following questions:

  • Where does John eat lunch?
  • What does he do when he arrives at home?

John works at Apple. Every morning, he wakes up early. He brushes his teeth and gets dressed in his Apple uniform. Then, he eats breakfast and leaves for work.

At work, John greets his colleagues with a smile. He checks his emails and starts his tasks for the day. Sometimes, he helps customers who come into the Apple store. He explains how to use the latest iPhones and MacBooks.

During lunchtime, John eats lunch with his coworkers in the Apple cafeteria. They talk about their favorite Apple products and share tips on how to use them better.

In the afternoon, John continues to work on his projects. He attends meetings with his team to discuss new ideas and plans. He also answers phone calls from customers who need assistance with their Apple devices.

Finally, when the workday is over, John says goodbye to his colleagues and heads home. He relaxes by watching some Apple TV or playing games on his iPhone before going to bed.

Internet security and passwords

Do you know what the term alphanumeric means?

Read the definition below:

"Alphanumeric" refers to a combination of alphabetic (letters) and numeric (numbers) characters. Alphanumeric characters are commonly used in passwords.

Inserir podcast

Podcast Title: "Your Guide to Internet Security"

Alex: Hi there, everyone! Welcome to our podcast today. I'm your host, Alex, and I'm here with my friend, Lisa.

Lisa: Hi, everyone! Today, we're going to talk about something important – keeping safe on the internet.

Alex: That's right, Lisa. So, let's start with the basics. Lisa, what is the internet?

Lisa: Well, the internet is like a big, virtual world where we can find and share information. We use it for everything, from chatting with friends to learning new things.

Alex: Exactly! And just like in the real world, we need to keep ourselves safe in this virtual world. One way to do that is by having strong passwords.

Lisa: Right! A password is like a secret key. It helps us get into our online accounts, like email or social media.

Alex: But Lisa, some people use simple passwords like "123456" or "password." That's not safe, is it?

Lisa: No, Alex, it's not. Using easy passwords is like leaving your front door wide open. We should use strong passwords that are hard for others to guess.

Alex: So, what makes a strong password?

Lisa: Good question! A strong password has a mix of letters (both uppercase and lowercase), numbers, and maybe some special characters.

Alex: Right, and we should avoid using personal information like our names or birthdays.

Lisa: Absolutely. Now, let's talk about another important thing – changing passwords regularly.

Alex: Why is that important, Lisa?

Lisa: Well, think of it like changing the lock on your front door. If you do it regularly, it's harder for someone to figure out how to get in.

Alex: Great analogy, Lisa! And for everyone listening, remember, don't share your passwords with anyone. It's like keeping your secret code to yourself.

Lisa: Exactly! And always log out when you're done using a website. It's like closing and locking the door when you leave your house.

Alex: That's some fantastic advice, Lisa. Thanks for sharing these important tips with us today.

Lisa: My pleasure, Alex! Let's all stay safe online and keep our virtual doors locked.

Alex: That's a wrap for today's podcast. Thanks for tuning in, and until next time, stay safe and secure on the internet!

ODBC

ODBC, ou Open Database Connectivity, é um conjunto de classes-modelo para conexão disponível em qualquer linguagem de programação, diferindo-se da JDBC, que é exclusiva para Java.

JDBC

JDBC, ou Java EE Database Connectivity, é uma API (application programming interfaces) que possibilita a interação do código Java com um banco de dados, na qual as instruções SQL são executadas por meio de um driver, composto por um grupo de classes e interfaces desenvolvido em Java. Esse processo possibilita a execução de instruções SQLs que se conectarão com a base de dados, permitindo a manipulação dos valores.
