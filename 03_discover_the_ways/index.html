The sentences above are examples of present continuous and present simple tenses. Do you know the difference between the simple present and present continuous? If you don’t, read the information in the chart.

SIMPLE PRESENT PRESENT CONTINUOUS

  • It is used to express general truths, habits and permanent states. For example:

  • The sky is blue. (general truth)

  • I always read a book in the morning. (habits)

  • I live in Brazil. (permanent states)

  • It is used to describe actions happening now and temporary states. For example:

  • She is reading a newspaper now. (happening now)

  • They are working at the museum until the end of this month. (temporary states)

  • Affirmative: (subject + verb (pay attention to the 3rd person.)

  • I, you, we, they work.

  • He, she, it works.

  • 3rd person rules:

Verbs ending in -y : the third person changes the -y to -ies:

fly -->flies, cry -->cries

Exception: if there is a vowel before the -y:

play -->plays, pray -->prays

Add -es to verbs ending in: -ss, -x, -sh, -ch and -o:

he passes, she catches, he fixes, it pushes

  • Affirmative: (subject + verb be + main verb + ing)

  • I am working
  • You are working
  • He’s working
  • She’s working
  • It’s working
  • We’re working
  • They’re working

  • Negative: (subject + auxiliary verb do/does + not + verb). (I, you, we, they don’t work)

(he, she, it doesn’t work)

  • Negative: (subject + verb be + not + main verb + ing)

  • I am not working
  • You aren’t working
  • He’s not working
  • She’s not working
  • It’s not working
  • We’re not working
  • They’re not working

  • Interrogative: (auxiliary verb do/does + subject + verb).

- Do you work?

- Does she work?

  • * Sometimes it includes information questions.

- Where do you work?

  • Interrogative: (Verb be + subject + main verb + ing)

  • Am I working?
  • Are you working?
  • Is he working?
  • Is she working?
  • Is it working?
  • Are we working?
  • Are they working?

  • * Sometimes it includes information questions.

- Where are you working?

Email

You have to write a formal email to your company. However, you need to learn about the basic concepts of an email that can help you writing it! Check these concepts divided in three sections: sending emails, receiving emails and managing messages.

SENDING EMAILS RECEIVING EMAILS MANAGING MESSAGES

Compose: Start by clicking on the "New Email" button, usually located prominently on your email platform.

Recipient: Enter the email address of the person or people you want to send the email to in the "To" field.

Subject: Write a concise and descriptive subject line that summarizes the content of your email.

Body: Type your message in the body of the email. You can format text, add attachments, or include links as needed.

Send: Once you're ready, click the "Send" button to send your email.

Inbox: When someone sends you an email, it will appear in your inbox.

Preview: You can see a preview of the email's content directly in your inbox.

Read: Click on the email to open and read its full contents.

Reply or Forward: If you want to respond to the email, you can click "Reply" to send a response to the sender or "Forward" to send it to someone else.

Organize: Use folders or labels to categorize your emails, such as "Work," "Personal," or "Important."

Mark as Unread: If you don't have time to respond immediately, you can mark emails as unread to remind yourself to respond later.

Archive or Delete: After reading an email, you can archive it to remove it from your inbox but keep it stored for future reference. Alternatively, you can delete emails you no longer need.

Search: Use the search function to find specific emails by sender, subject, or keywords.

Manage Attachments: Save or download attachments from emails to your computer if needed.

Stay Organized: Regularly clean up your inbox by archiving or deleting old emails to keep it clutter-free.

ODBC

ODBC, ou Open Database Connectivity, é um conjunto de classes-modelo para conexão disponível em qualquer linguagem de programação, diferindo-se da JDBC, que é exclusiva para Java.

JDBC

JDBC, ou Java EE Database Connectivity, é uma API (application programming interfaces) que possibilita a interação do código Java com um banco de dados, na qual as instruções SQL são executadas por meio de um driver, composto por um grupo de classes e interfaces desenvolvido em Java. Esse processo possibilita a execução de instruções SQLs que se conectarão com a base de dados, permitindo a manipulação dos valores.
